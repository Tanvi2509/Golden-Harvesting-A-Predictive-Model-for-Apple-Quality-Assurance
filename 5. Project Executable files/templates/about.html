1. Data Collection & Preparation

The first step involves collecting the dataset, which includes gathering data on various apple quality metrics such as size, color, and firmness from different sources. Once the data is collected, it undergoes preparation by addressing missing values, selecting relevant features, encoding categorical variables, and normalizing or standardizing the data.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis

During Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), descriptive statistics are computed to understand the distribution of the data. This includes calculating mean, median, and standard deviation for different features. Visual analysis is then performed using plots such as histograms and scatter plots to examine distributions and relationships between features.

3. Model Building

Model building involves training the model using multiple algorithms to predict apple quality. Various algorithms, such as Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and Random Forests, are applied to the data. After training, the model is tested to evaluate its performance based on metrics like accuracy, precision, and recall.

4. Performance Testing & Hyperparameter Tuning

The performance of the model is assessed using multiple evaluation metrics to ensure it effectively predicts apple quality. Hyperparameter tuning is conducted to optimize the model's parameters, and the accuracy of the model is compared before and after tuning to measure improvements.

5. Model Deployment

In the deployment phase, the best-performing model is saved for future use. It is then integrated into a web framework, such as Flask or Django, allowing users to input apple features and receive quality predictions in real-time through a user-friendly interface.
